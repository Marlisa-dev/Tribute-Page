Maya Angelou

American Poet - (1928 - 2014)

"Love recognizes no barriers. It jumps hurdles, leaps fences, penetrates walls to arrive at its destination full of hope." - Maya Angelou

Timeline of Maya Angelou's Life

Origin Story

  • 1928 - Born in St Louis as the second child to a Navy dieticin and a nurse
  • 1931 - Her parent's marriage ended and she was sent to live with her paternal grandmother in Arkansas
  • 1935 - She and her brother were returned to the care of her mother in St. Louis
  • 1937 - Maya was sexually assaulted by her mother's boyfriend. The trauma from this experience made her mute and the children were sent back to live with their grnadmother again.
  • 1942 - Maya sttended schol here her Teacher, Bertha Flowers helped Maya speak again and inspired her interest in literature
  • 1942 - She dropped out of school and became the first African American female cable car conductor in San Franscisco.

Early Career

  • 1952 - She first began her career as a nightclob singer
  • 1957 - Her first albun called Calypso lady was recorded
  • 1958 - Moved to New York and joined the Harlen Writers Guild
  • 1959 - Became a Civil Right Activist
  • 1960 - Travelled and met Civil Right Activists from around the world including countries like Egypt and Ghana
  • 1964 - Returned to the US to help Malcom X build host Organization of African American Unity
  • 1968 - After the assissination of Malcolm X, she began working on her Autobiography 'I know Why the Caged Bird Sings'

Publishing Career & Later Life

  • 1970 - I know Why the Caged Bird Sings is published
  • 1977 - Appeared as a supporting role for the tv mini-series Roots
  • 1981 - The Heart of the Woman is published
  • 1993 - At the inauguration of President Bill Clinton, Maya Angelou reads her poem On the Pulse of Morning
  • 2002 - Published A Song Flung Up to Heaven
  • 2006 - Receives Mother Theresa Award
  • 2008 - Become the first recipeint of the Hope for Peace and Justice Voice of Peace Award
  • 2010 - Amgelou domnated her personal papers and career memorabilia to the Schomburg Center for Research in Black Cultre in Harlem
  • 2013 - She publihsed the seventh volume of autobiography in her series which focuseed on her relationship with her mother titled Mom & Me & Mom.
  • 2014 - Maya Angelou died on May 28, 2014 at the age of 86
